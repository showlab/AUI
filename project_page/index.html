Abstract

Computer-Use Agents (CUAs) are increasingly capable of operating complex graphical user interfaces, yet most digital environments remain tailored for humans. Aesthetic-heavy layouts, hidden interactions, and manually triggered workflows hinder agents from executing tasks efficiently.

We present AUI, a framework that rethinks UI creation for agents by pairing a coding-oriented Coder with an autonomous CUA Judge. The Coder iteratively designs interfaces while the CUA probes them through task-solving trajectories. AUI introduces the AUI-Gym benchmark spanning 52 applications across six domains and proposes the CUA Dashboard, a compact visualization that condenses multi-step rollouts into actionable feedback.

Across state-of-the-art proprietary and open Coders—and multiple CUAs—AUI substantially improves both functionality completeness and navigation success. Our agent-as-judge paradigm shifts interface design toward agent-native reliability and paves the way for scalable automatic GUI development.
